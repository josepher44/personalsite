Here’s a look at the overall approach, and some of the detailed mechanisms, I see at this time as being the most promising approaches to base the physical implementation of the LEGOSortus around. This is all extremely subject to change, and I don’t anticipate actually building many of the systems here for a bit until the part flow analysis is more thoroughly done out, particularly the systems downstream of the identification cells. Again, high level ideas of part flow and software capabilities hold absolute control over the mechanical system. That being said…I think the system is pretty well thought-through, and does a decent job at taking on a lot of the challenges I’ve talked about. At this point, it is likely that things learned through software and data analysis will impact the scale and configuration of certain elements, rather than forcing changes in the conceptual design of the elements themselves. Still, there isn’t much sense in getting into detailed, nut-and-bolt level CAD. Most of the models here were built for documentation rather than development purposes, and otherwise do their job just as well simply floating around my head.

Again, the core operating principle here is, to target a three dimensional storage solution, by breaking the post-identification sort into three phases, each corresponding to the X, Y, and Z axis of the final storage solution. Prior to this, the machine also needs to go from a mixed pile of LEGO, into a form more suitably passed through a carefully controlled vision cell for identification. And at the end, in the stored form, the parts have to be at least reasonably accessible, to allow them to be processed as orders are placed.

And, again, what I’m presenting here is the end-game for this project. It’s going to be a long, incremental road to go before I’m in a position to make this physically real at the scale I’m pitching here. But I have to be aware of what it needs to look like at the end. That way, my incremental progress, testing small pieces of the system, and putting it to limited use in processing and selling subsets of parts or augmenting the manual sorting process, can be done in a way which feeds into the eventual creation of this gigantic, absurd machine.
